UX Design · Mobile App · Hybrid App

WKDK — IMPROVING UX FLOW OF
SAVING PHOTOS ON HYBRID MOBILE APP

Role Product Designer, Visual Designer, UX Researcher
Duration April 2022 — June 2022
Company WKDK.kr

Overview

Overview

WKDK is simplified store management mobile app with smart checklists by photo record. The product team aimed to focus on improving convenience in store managing for both managers and workers.

Contribution

Collaborated with the product team from brief to delivery, contributing UI decisions across brand, components, and screen design. And furthermore, executed UX research, casual user test.

Context & Brief

01
Better UX Flow
had been required

As the number of users have been increased, new features were added and old UX flow was not properly fit on the app.

It caused following errors, such as misunderstanding of the features, and server errors from the misunderstanding.

02
Expanding
B2B Users

The team was discussing with ADT Cap. to introduce our app to their security tasks.

That made us to optimize UX configurations for onsite personnel and subcontractors, prioritizing quick task completion and minimizing cognitive load in fast-paced work settings.

Timeline

Painpoint

" Too long to complete my task in a minute! "

The biggest problem lay in the disconnect between the actual field where the app is used and the app experience itself.

"

We need to attach multiple photos for 1 task.

Multiple photos verification account for, approximately 40% in total cases.

"

But I'm lost while I tried to attach photos.

We received 3–5 VOCs about photo verification per a month. The bound rate on "Select attachment method" was recorded 70%.

Solution

Shorten photo verification flow

Giving an easier user experience on photo verification flow is determined to a new solution.

Comparative Usability Test

Our team had a concern of usability on "how do users think attaching photos feature on a camera screen". So I made low-fidelity wireframe to implement a casual usability test to co-workers, who were not involved with this project.

Scenario A

The similar draft that has an editing page after camera.

Scenario B

Attaching photos on camera interface.

Purpose

Can users accept the 'image editing features' on the camera screen?

Testers

4 coworkers who are not involved with this project / 2 Brwnie* crews

*Brwnie: unmanned store management service by Higher X (WKDK)

Q1

Which one was the better option for processing tasks easier and faster?

Q2

Did you feel awkward in the flow of B? Which part?

Q3

How do you feel about editing photos on the camera screen — positive or negative?

Q4

Is A's editing page, which appears after the camera, necessary?

Results

Mostly positive about the new feature.
So we decided to shorten the photo verification flow.

1

All testers found Scenario B easier and faster for task completion.

2

No one felt awkward in the flow of the camera-integrated editing.

3

Most testers responded positively to editing photos directly on camera screen.

Interfaces

Shorten flow supports hectic frontlines

We decided to give integrated features, editing and taking photos on camera screen at the same time. This offers a faster flow while they're uploading photo verification to users, and it eventually leads to more frequent uploading on the app.

Result

" Activating core features & expanding the user base "

The new feature has been recorded as a successful update, even if it had been implemented in an unexpected local environment.

25% increased of photo verification cases

Following the update, the daily average of verification photos uploaded increased by over 120, a 25% jump from before. This serves as a positive indicator that users are engaging with the service more actively and flexibly.

A solid groundwork for B2B clients

We successfully secured a new B2B client, ADT, following this update, which paved the way for expansion into various industries, such as property management and outsourced management. It laid a solid foundation for driving user growth in the second half of the year.
